We Have Been Harmonised examines how the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) maintains power not only through surveillance and coercion, but through psychological conditioning, language control, and cultural compliance. Strittmatter argues that modern authoritarianism in China functions less through brute force and more through a vast, self-correcting apparatus that encourages citizens to internalise acceptable behaviour.
The book explores the legacy of the Tiananmen massacre, the role of party cadres, ideological flexibility disguised as pragmatism, and how Marxist rhetoric has evolved into a tool for control rather than liberation.
